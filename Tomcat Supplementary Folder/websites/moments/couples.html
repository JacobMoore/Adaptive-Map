Couples

A couple in engineering mechanics is a set of parallel forces with equal magnitudes but opposite directions like the forces shown in the diagram below. Each of these forces will exert a moment, just as in every system, but there is something special about the combination of these two forces.

A couple.

We will start by finding the moment about point (P) using the process outlined on the "Moment about a Point" page. The two forces in the couple (F1 and F2) have the same magnitude (F), they have parallel but opposite directions separated by a distance d. The scalar equations for the moment we get:

\[ \mathrm{ M_{F1}= -F * x }\]

and

\[ \mathrm{ M_{F2}= F * (x + d) }\]

if we add the two moments together to get the total moment exerted we get the following formula

\[ \mathrm{ M_{Total}= (-F * x) + (F * (x + d)) }\]

which we can simplify to

\[ \mathrm{ M_{Total}= -F*x + F*x + F*d }\]
\[ \mathrm{ M_{Total}= F*d }\]

As you can see, we've eliminated the value x from the equation entirely. If we choose another point P with a different value for "x" it doesn't matter; the moment exerted by the couple will still be (F * d). This means that couples exert the same moment on all parts of a body. This makes them different from non-couple forces where the moment exerted by the force or forces is dependent on the point the moment is being taken about.

When representing the moment of a couple as a vector, the moment vector will have a magnitude, axis of rotation and direction of rotation like any other moment vector. The magnitude of the vector is equal to F*d, the direction of the vector is perpendicular to the plane the two parallel forces are in. You can use the right hand to determine which direction the moment takes. The moment vector of a couple can be translated to any point (as long as direction and magnitude are preserved) without changing the system, unlike most moment vectors.

A moment exerted by a couple can be moved anywhere on the body, but a moment exerted by a force or set of forces that is not a couple cannot be moved from the point which was used to calculate the moments.
